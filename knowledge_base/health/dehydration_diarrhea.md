# Diarrhea and dehydration

Diarrhea is passing loose or watery stools three or more times a day. The biggest danger, especially for children, is dehydration — losing more water and salts than you take in.

## Signs of dehydration
- Thirst, dry mouth and tongue
- Passing little or dark urine
- Sunken eyes; in babies, a sunken soft spot on the head
- Skin that stays pinched instead of springing back
- Weakness, dizziness, or drowsiness
- In babies: crying without tears, fewer wet diapers

## Treatment at home
The most important treatment is oral rehydration solution (ORS).

If ORS packets are available, mix one packet with the stated amount of clean water and drink frequently in small sips.

Homemade ORS: mix 6 level teaspoons of sugar and half a level teaspoon of salt into 1 litre of clean (boiled and cooled) water. Taste it — it should be no saltier than tears.

- Keep drinking after every loose stool: adults about one cup, children smaller sips
- Continue breastfeeding babies as usual
- Keep eating light food when possible; do not fast
- Zinc supplements for children (10-14 days) shorten diarrhea; use if available

Do NOT use sugary sodas or undiluted fruit juice as a rehydration drink — they can worsen diarrhea.

## Seek medical help urgently if
- Signs of severe dehydration (very drowsy, unable to drink, no urine)
- Blood in the stool
- High fever with diarrhea
- Diarrhea lasting more than 2 days in a child or 3 days in an adult
- The person is a baby under 6 months, elderly, pregnant, or has a chronic illness
