# Feeding babies and children well

## Babies 0-6 months
Breast milk alone is the best food for the first 6 months — no water, teas, or other foods are needed, even in hot weather. Feed on demand, day and night. Exclusive breastfeeding protects against diarrhea and infections.

## Babies 6-24 months
- From 6 months, continue breastfeeding AND start soft foods: mashed rice, porridge, mashed vegetables, egg, beans, fruit
- Increase variety, amount, and thickness as the child grows
- Feed young children patiently from their own bowl so you can see how much they eat
- Add a little oil, egg, ground nuts, beans, or fish to porridge to make it more nourishing

## Signs of malnutrition in children
- Weight loss or no weight gain over months; the child looks thin, with loose skin
- Swollen feet or belly with thin arms (severe — needs a clinic urgently)
- Hair becoming thin, pale, or falling out
- The child is weak, inactive, or stops playing
- Getting sick often and recovering slowly

A child with a mid-upper-arm that looks very thin, or who is too weak to eat, must be seen at a clinic immediately — severe malnutrition is a medical emergency.

## Simple rules for all children
- Wash hands with soap before preparing food and before feeding
- Use clean (boiled/treated) water for drinking and mixing food
- Offer vegetables and fruit daily when available; limit sweets and sugary drinks
- Sick children need MORE food and fluids, not less — keep feeding through illness and give extra meals while recovering
