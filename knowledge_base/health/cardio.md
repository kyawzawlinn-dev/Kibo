# Cardiology – General Knowledge

## What is Hypertrophy?
Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH) is a thickening of the heart muscle.  
Common causes include hypertension and athletic training.

## Ejection Fraction (EF)
- EF measures how much blood the left ventricle pumps.
- Normal EF: 55–70%
- Mildly reduced: 40–55%
- Severely reduced: < 40%

## When to Seek Help
Seek urgent evaluation if symptoms include:
- Chest discomfort
- Shortness of breath at rest
- Fainting or dizziness
