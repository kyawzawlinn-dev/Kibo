# Pregnancy: healthy habits and danger signs

## Everyday care during pregnancy
- Attend antenatal check-ups — at least 4, ideally 8 visits during the pregnancy
- Eat varied food: vegetables, fruit, eggs, beans, fish or meat when available
- Take iron and folic acid supplements if provided by the clinic
- Sleep under a bed net in malaria areas; malaria in pregnancy is dangerous
- Avoid alcohol, tobacco, and smoke-filled rooms
- Check with a health worker before taking any medicine
- Rest more than usual and drink plenty of clean water

## Common discomforts that are usually normal
Mild nausea in early months, heartburn, backache, swollen ankles at the end of the day, and more frequent urination are common. Small frequent meals help nausea; resting with feet raised helps swelling.

## Danger signs — go to a clinic or hospital immediately
- Bleeding from the vagina, at any stage
- Severe headache, blurred vision, or swelling of the face and hands (possible pre-eclampsia)
- Fits or convulsions
- High fever
- Severe belly pain
- The baby stops moving or moves much less than usual
- Waters break long before labour, or fluid is green/brown
- Labour pains before 8 months

## Planning for the birth
- Plan to deliver with a skilled birth attendant — at a clinic or hospital where possible
- Arrange transport in advance in case of emergency
- Prepare clean cloths, and money set aside for emergencies
- After birth: early and exclusive breastfeeding protects the baby; bleeding heavier than a normal period is a danger sign for the mother
