# Safe water and food hygiene

Many illnesses — diarrhea, typhoid, cholera, hepatitis A — spread through unsafe water and food. Clean habits prevent most of them.

## Making water safe to drink
- Boiling is the most reliable method: bring water to a rolling boil for at least 1 minute, then let it cool covered
- Chlorine tablets or drops: follow the package instructions and wait the stated time before drinking
- Solar disinfection: fill clear plastic bottles and leave them in full sun for at least 6 hours
- Store treated water in a clean, covered container; pour it out rather than dipping cups or hands into it

Cloudy water should be filtered through a clean cloth before boiling or treating.

## Food hygiene basics
- Wash hands with soap before cooking, before eating, and after using the toilet or changing a baby
- Cook meat, fish, and eggs thoroughly; eat food while it is hot
- Do not leave cooked food at room temperature for more than 2 hours; reheat until steaming before eating
- Wash fruits and vegetables with safe water, or peel them
- Keep raw meat separate from other food; wash knives and boards after cutting raw meat
- Protect food from flies, rodents, and dust — keep it covered

## During an outbreak of diarrhea or cholera in your area
- Drink and cook only with boiled or treated water
- Avoid raw food, ice, and street-prepared drinks
- Wash hands with soap even more often
- Anyone with heavy watery diarrhea should start oral rehydration solution immediately and get to a clinic
