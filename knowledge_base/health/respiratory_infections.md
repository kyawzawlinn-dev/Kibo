# Coughs, colds, flu, and pneumonia

Most coughs and colds are caused by viruses and get better on their own within 1-2 weeks. Antibiotics do not help viral infections.

## Home care for colds and flu
- Rest and drink plenty of warm fluids
- Paracetamol can ease fever, headache, and body aches (follow package dosing)
- Honey (for adults and children over 1 year) soothes coughs; never give honey to babies under 1
- Salt-water gargles help a sore throat
- Keep rooms ventilated; avoid smoke, which worsens coughs

## Flu vs a cold
Flu comes on suddenly with fever, chills, body aches, and exhaustion; a cold builds gradually with sneezing, runny nose, and sore throat. Both are managed the same way at home.

## Pneumonia — when a chest infection turns serious
Pneumonia is an infection deep in the lungs. It can follow a cold or flu and needs medical treatment, often antibiotics.

Warning signs in adults:
- Fast or difficult breathing, breathlessness at rest
- Chest pain when breathing or coughing
- Coughing up rust-coloured or bloody phlegm
- High fever with shaking chills, or confusion in older people

Warning signs in children:
- Fast breathing or the chest pulling in below the ribs with each breath
- Grunting, nostrils flaring, blue lips or face
- Unable to drink or breastfeed, unusually sleepy

Any of these signs means see a health worker urgently.

## A cough lasting more than 2-3 weeks
A cough that will not go away — especially with night sweats, weight loss, or blood in the phlegm — could be tuberculosis (TB). TB is curable with free treatment in most countries, but it must be diagnosed at a clinic. Get tested; do not wait.
