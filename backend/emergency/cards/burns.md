# Burns and scalds
keywords: burned my, burnt my, scald, burn injury, got burned, got burnt, boiling water on

1. Cool the burn under cool running water for 20 minutes. Do not use ice.
2. Remove rings and loose clothing near the burn before it swells — but never peel off clothing stuck to the skin.
3. Cover the burn loosely with clean plastic wrap or a clean non-fluffy cloth.
4. Do NOT apply butter, oil, toothpaste, or creams.
5. Do NOT burst blisters.
6. Offer small sips of water.

Seek medical care urgently if the burn is larger than the person's palm, on the face, hands, or genitals, looks deep, white, or charred — or if the person is a young child or elderly.
