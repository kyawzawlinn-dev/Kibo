# High fever in babies and children
keywords: baby has a fever, baby fever, infant fever, child has a high fever, my child has a fever, baby is burning up

1. Undress the child to light clothing and keep the room comfortably cool.
2. Offer plenty of fluids in small, frequent sips (breast milk or water).
3. You may give children's paracetamol or ibuprofen at the correct dose for their age and weight. Never give aspirin to children.
4. Do NOT use cold baths or alcohol rubs.
5. Check on the child often, including during the night.

Seek medical help URGENTLY if the child: is under 3 months old with any fever, has a stiff neck or a rash that does not fade when pressed, struggles to breathe, is unusually drowsy or hard to wake, has a seizure, shows signs of dehydration (no wet diapers, sunken eyes), or the fever lasts more than 3 days.
