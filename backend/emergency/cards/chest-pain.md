# Chest pain / suspected heart attack
keywords: chest pain, heart attack, chest tightness, pain spreading to my arm, pain in my chest

1. Get emergency help immediately — do not wait to see if it passes.
2. Help the person sit down, knees bent, head and shoulders supported. Stop all activity.
3. Loosen tight clothing.
4. If they are not allergic to it and can swallow, they may slowly chew one adult aspirin (300 mg).
5. Do not leave them alone. Stay calm and reassure them.
6. If they become unresponsive and stop breathing normally, start CPR: push hard and fast in the centre of the chest, about 2 pushes per second, until help arrives.

Warning signs: pressure or squeezing in the chest, pain spreading to the arm, jaw, or back, cold sweat, nausea, shortness of breath.
